# Vision

## Scope

This file describes the durable direction for LVTD and `lvtd.dev`. It should
change less often than page copy or implementation details.

## North Star

LVTD should become the parent brand for a portfolio of useful software projects
and an AI-native product engineering service that founders can hire when they
need working systems, not strategy theater.

The website should make LVTD feel like:

- A builder-led studio with real shipped projects.
- A practical AI services partner for messy product, automation, data, agent, and
  infrastructure work.
- A service-as-software organization that packages reusable code, agent
  workflows, deployment patterns, and operating playbooks into faster client
  outcomes.

## Strategic Posture

Lean into agency as a service:

- Sell concrete outcomes and implementation paths, not vague consulting time.
- Make it obvious what LVTD handles and where the client's responsibility begins.
- Keep a human accountable for quality, security, operations, and delivery.
- Use deposits, named offers, and follow-up workflows to reduce sales friction.

Lean into service-as-software:

- Treat internal tools, agent skills, Django templates, deployment patterns, and
  project infrastructure as leverage.
- Reuse proven parts across client work when they improve quality or speed.
- Show enough of the machinery that technical buyers trust the service.
- Let projects and open-source work act as proof of the operating system behind
  the service.

Keep building products:

- LVTD projects are proof, distribution, reusable assets, and experiments.
- Project work should support the service narrative when possible.
- Do not make the site feel like a passive portfolio. The business should be
  hireable.

## Product Future

Over time, `lvtd.dev` should support:

- A clearer services architecture for MVP builds, AI workflow implementation,
  internal tools, product rescue, and managed infrastructure.
- Service pages with best-fit customer, scope, process, proof, pricing or deposit
  signals, and clear next steps.
- Writing that teaches from actual shipped work and attracts founders with
  concrete problems.
- Project pages or case-study surfaces that connect shipped work to service
  credibility.
- Lightweight intake, payment, and follow-up flows that make serious inquiries
  easy to start.

## What Should Never Drift

- Specificity beats breadth.
- Working software beats decks.
- Human accountability beats AI hype.
- Founder-builder voice beats agency polish.
- Proof beats claims.
- Useful projects remain a core part of the brand.

## Non-Goals

- Do not become a generic full-service agency site.
- Do not bury Rasul/LVTD's builder identity behind corporate language.
- Do not present AI as magic or as a replacement for engineering judgment.
- Do not add enterprise-heavy process language unless the actual offer requires
  it.
- Do not let visual polish obscure the practical service path.

## Strategy Source

The current strategic direction is informed by internal research around SaaS,
service-as-software, and agency-as-a-service. If a task requires details from
that research beyond this steering file, ask for an exported copy or pasted
