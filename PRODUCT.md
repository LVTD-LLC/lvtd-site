# Product

## Scope

This file is the product context for `lvtd-site`, the public website for LVTD,
LLC. Use it when making copy, page-structure, offer, SEO, conversion, or product
positioning decisions.

## Positioning

LVTD is both a product lab and an AI-native product engineering service. The site
should make two things true at the same time:

1. LVTD ships its own useful projects, tools, and experiments.
2. LVTD can be hired to turn messy product, automation, AI workflow, and
   infrastructure problems into working software.

The service angle should lean into "agency as a service" and
"service-as-software": accountable human engineering backed by reusable
software, agent workflows, deployment patterns, and project playbooks. Do not
position LVTD as a generic design agency, pure SaaS company, or abstract AI
consultancy.

## Users

Primary users:

- Founders and small teams evaluating whether to hire Rasul/LVTD for hands-on
  product engineering, automation, AI workflow implementation, debugging,
  infrastructure, or hosted OpenClaw support.
- Operators with a painful manual workflow, spreadsheet, internal process,
  content system, or stalled product that needs a working implementation.
- Technical founders who want a senior builder to help scope, ship, and improve
  a first useful version without hiring a full internal team.

Secondary users:

- People browsing LVTD projects for credibility, ideas, or open-source work.
- Potential collaborators, customers, and clients who need to understand the
  parent organization behind the projects.

Users are usually scanning quickly for credibility, service fit, proof of shipped
work, price or deposit signals, and a low-friction way to start.

## Product Purpose

`lvtd.dev` explains what LVTD builds, shows practical proof through projects and
writing, and converts qualified visitors into project inquiries or paid deposits.

Success means the site feels credible, specific, technically capable, and easy to
act on. It should sound like a capable engineer who can step into unclear product
work and move it forward, not like a template SaaS brand or a vague growth shop.

## Offer Architecture

Current offers and surfaces:

- General product engineering: tools, sites, apps, MVPs, automations, audits,
  rescue work, and launch support.
- MVP done for you: a deposit-led, focused build offer with a visible final price
  signal.
- Hosted OpenClaw: managed OpenClaw deployment, operations, monitoring,
  security, integrations, and ongoing iteration.
- Writing and project pages: credibility, proof, and source material for future
  service pages.

When adding new offers, make them concrete. Prefer a named outcome, best-fit
customer, what LVTD handles, starting path, and proof points. Avoid vague
"AI transformation" or "growth partner" framing.

## What Good Looks Like

- The homepage quickly answers what LVTD does, why it is credible, and how to
  start.
- Projects are not decoration. They are evidence of taste, technical range, and
  shipping momentum.
- Service pages explain the operational burden LVTD removes and the working
  system the client receives.
- CTAs are direct: start a build, reserve a deposit, ask a question, view work,
  or read notes.
- Copy stays specific about workflows, data, deployment, operations, AI agents,
  and software outcomes.

## In Scope

- Public marketing and service pages for LVTD and productized services.
- Blog and writing surfaces that support trust, SEO, and founder education.
- Payment/deposit flows for services that have clear fulfillment expectations.
- SEO metadata, structured data, sitemap, robots, and legal pages.
- Proof-oriented project listings and links to LVTD properties.

## Out Of Scope

- A generic agency landing page with broad capabilities and weak proof.
- A pure portfolio site that hides the ability to hire LVTD.
- A pure SaaS dashboard or authenticated app inside this repository.
- Claims that imply fully autonomous AI delivery without human accountability.
- Copy that overpromises timelines, revenue, model performance, or legal/security
  outcomes.

## Brand Personality

Practical expert: direct, technically confident, founder-friendly, and biased
toward shipping. The site should feel like a builder who has handled real
systems, can make scope decisions, and cares about finished edges.

## Anti-References

Avoid generic SaaS and agency tropes: repeated decorative card grids, vague
growth claims, interchangeable service copy, stock startup language, gratuitous
gradients, and excessive uppercase labels.

Also avoid designs that become so sparse they feel unfinished, or so flashy that
credibility takes a back seat to ornament.

## Product Principles

1. Make trust concrete: pair claims with projects, writing, process, code,
   pricing, or operating details.
2. Keep the founder-to-founder voice: clear language, visible contact paths, and
   minimal ceremony.
3. Give each section one job: services, proof, writing, and payment CTAs should
   not blur together.
4. Convert through specificity: users should know what LVTD will do and what the
   next step means.
5. Let reusable assets show through: agent skills, Django patterns, deployment
   playbooks, and product infrastructure can be proof of the
   service-as-software approach.

## Accessibility And Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus states, readable line lengths,
reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that preserve hierarchy on
mobile. Theme behavior should be consistent across all pages.
