# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders, small teams, and internet business operators evaluating whether to hire Rasul/LVTD for hands-on product engineering, automation, debugging, or hosted OpenClaw support. They are likely scanning quickly for credibility, service fit, proof of shipped work, and a low-friction way to start a conversation or make a deposit.

## Product Purpose

LVTD is a consulting and product-engineering site that explains what Rasul builds, shows practical proof through projects and writing, and converts qualified visitors into project inquiries or paid deposits. Success means the site feels credible, specific, technically capable, and easy to act on without sounding like a generic agency landing page.

## Brand Personality

Practical expert: direct, technically confident, founder-friendly, and biased toward shipping. The site should feel like a capable engineer who can step into messy product work and move it forward quickly, not like a template SaaS brand or a vague growth consultancy.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS and agency tropes: repeated card grids, vague growth claims, decorative gradients, interchangeable service copy, excessive uppercase section labels, and overly polished stock startup language. Also avoid designs that become so sparse they feel unfinished, or so flashy that credibility takes a back seat to ornament.

## Design Principles

1. Make trust concrete: pair service claims with specific proof, projects, writing, or process details.
2. Preserve founder-to-founder directness: use clear language, visible contact paths, and minimal ceremony.
3. Give each section a distinct job: services, proof, writing, and payment CTAs should not blur together.
4. Use restraint with intent: quiet design is acceptable only when hierarchy, rhythm, and specificity carry the page.
5. Keep conversion paths obvious: inquiry, blog, product links, and deposits should be visually differentiated by intent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus states, readable line lengths, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that preserve hierarchy on mobile. Theme behavior should be consistent across all pages.
